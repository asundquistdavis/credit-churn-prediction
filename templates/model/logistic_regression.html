To test our dataset, we also used Logistic Regression. Here were the results:

Training Data Score: 0.8957208689927584

Testing Data Score: 0.8969194312796208

This is not really ideal but it shows that our model is decent. A great model would look like a vertically flipped L. If you’re trying to identify customers that will leave soon, you could use this model to send them offers they might accept, to keep their business. It’s not perfect, but it could help you.

AUC for the Logistic Regression Model: 0.7311381590021302

This is an acceptable AUC but it is not that great. It means that 73% of our ROC curve is below the line. If we care a lot about model quality, we’d pursue something 90% or higher.

As you can see the model has a serious false positive problem. However, if you’re prioritized on keeping customers, you might find this acceptable, as it could be better to give a special offer to someone who was going to stay on anyway than to ignore someone who was on the brink of leaving.
